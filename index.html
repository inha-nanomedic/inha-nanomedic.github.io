About the Lab

Healthy Research
for Your Health

The Integrated Research Lab for Medicinal Nano and Life Science at Inha University College of Medicine has been at the forefront of biomedical innovation for over two decades. Our interdisciplinary team brings together expertise in pharmaceutical science, materials engineering, and clinical medicine to develop breakthrough therapeutic solutions.

We specialize in designing advanced drug delivery systems that overcome the limitations of conventional treatments. From nano-scale carriers that target specific tissues to bio-adhesive hydrogels that revolutionize wound care, our research translates fundamental science into tangible medical impact.

Guided by a commitment to patient-centered innovation, we collaborate with clinical partners, industry leaders, and international research institutions to accelerate the journey from laboratory discovery to bedside application.

Learn More
Nanomedicine & Formulation

Engineering nano-scale drug carriers and advanced pharmaceutical formulations for targeted, controlled-release therapeutics with enhanced bioavailability.

Drug Delivery Systems

Developing novel oral, injectable, and implantable delivery platforms for proteins, peptides, and nucleic acids to address unmet clinical needs.

Biomaterial Engineering

Creating biocompatible materials for medical devices, tissue scaffolds, and hemostatic agents that integrate seamlessly with biological systems.

Research Areas

Scientific Focus

Our six core research programs tackle critical challenges in drug delivery, biomaterials, and therapeutic innovation.

01

Bio-adhesive Hydrogel for Hemostasis

Developing advanced bio-adhesive hydrogel systems that rapidly control bleeding and promote wound healing through tissue-specific adhesion mechanisms and biocompatible cross-linking strategies.

Hydrogel Hemostasis Wound Healing
02

Protein/Peptide Oral Delivery

Engineering oral delivery platforms that protect therapeutic proteins and peptides from gastrointestinal degradation, enhancing absorption and systemic bioavailability for patient-friendly treatments.

Oral Delivery Peptide Bioavailability
03

miRNA Delivery for Cancer Therapy

Designing nanoparticle-based miRNA delivery systems that selectively target tumor cells, modulating gene expression to suppress cancer progression and overcome therapeutic resistance.

miRNA Cancer Gene Therapy
