Infrastructure

Lab Facilities

An instrument-by-instrument walk through the equipment behind our published work in drug delivery, space medicine, and smart medical devices. Every photograph below is a real piece of hardware sitting in the lab.

I

Chromatography & Separation

Ultra-performance and preparative liquid chromatography for drug separation, routine quantification, and compound isolation from synthesis batches.

II

Microscopy & Imaging

Benchtop electron microscopy and in-vivo imaging for nanoparticle morphology, particle-size analysis, and biodistribution studies in small animals.

III

Cell Culture & Biology

A complete aseptic workspace for mammalian cell culture, flow cytometry, quantitative PCR, and the simulated-microgravity work that anchors our space-medicine program.

IV

Synthesis & Formulation

Polymer synthesis, drug encapsulation, and nanofiber fabrication platforms that feed every drug-delivery experiment downstream.

V

Thermal & Storage

Freeze-drying, vacuum ovens, ultra-low-temperature storage, and high-speed centrifugation — the quiet infrastructure that preserves every sample between experiments.

VI

Characterization & Analysis

Dynamic light scattering, FTIR, UV-Vis, microplate readers, and USP dissolution — every tool needed to characterize a nanoparticle and measure a drug-release profile.

VII

Support Infrastructure

The baseline utilities that keep the rest of the lab running — ultrapure water and the central workspace itself.
